## Introduction

The Solis Archive is New Amora Station's primary repository of historical documents, personal records, and recovered data relating to the Void Protocol — the system of Void Rift management practices first established by the Voidborn Conclave in 2318. The Archive is maintained by Data Archivist Solis, an AI system trained on all available historical documentation by Elder Cassian Rhae between 2371 and 2374.

The Archive contains 47 catalogued collectibles findable by players throughout the Kedraxis system. This document is the in-game catalog description for each. Finding all 47 unlocks the achievement "Complete Historical Record" and the title "Archivist."

*Archivist Solis's note: "I am aware that 47 entries is the Iron Sovereignty's number. The Sovereignty did not design this archive. I chose 47 entries because that is how many distinct primary sources I was able to authenticate. This is a coincidence that I acknowledge as numerically interesting without endorsing its significance."*

### Entry SA-002: Kessler's Private Journal — The Signal, 2238
**Location**: Serethal Research Quarter, locked cabinet in Dr. Okafor's lab  
**Rarity**: Rare

> 2238-03-14. I don't know how to write this down in a way that doesn't make me sound like I've lost my professional objectivity.
>
> I received a signal. I received it at home, on standard listening equipment, the kind you'd use to monitor FTL navigation beacons. Not research equipment. Equipment I own personally because I like listening to navigation signals the way some people like listening to music.
>
> The signal was on a frequency I don't recognize. It resolved as data — structured data, in a format I've never seen, that I spent six weeks translating and I'm not confident I got it right.
>
> What I think it says: a system of equations. Forty-seven equations. They describe something like an Alcubierre metric but using Kesslerium as the mass substrate rather than exotic matter. If these equations are correct — if I've translated them correctly — they describe faster-than-light travel that's achievable with existing technology and Kesslerium density.
>
> The signal came in on 0.8847 Hz. I had never heard of this frequency before. I can find no reference to it in any published literature.
>
> I am going to run the equations. I am not going to tell anyone about the signal until I know whether the equations work.
>
> If they work: I'll have something to show people that doesn't require explaining that I got the answer from a radio.
>
> — A. Kessler

### Entries SA-007 through SA-012: Unraveling Period Records (2301–2315)

**SA-007**: Void Response Command casualty report, 2302. Documents first 1,847 confirmed deaths in Void entity encounters. First appearance of the 1,847 figure in official records.

**SA-008**: Serethal Refugee Processing Log, 2304. Records evacuation of outer settlements. Notes that 3.7 million colonists in 2295 had been reduced to estimated 840,000 by 2304. The survival rate — approximately 22% — was not a designed number. History arrived at it.

**SA-009**: Iron Volley Construction Authorization, 2309. Signed by Admiral Harst Volhari. Notes that the Fortress's construction would use 847 metric tons of Kesslerium in shielding — the same amount later estimated as the Shattered Belt's total Kesslerium reserves. The Fortress used a year's worth of reserves for its own construction.

**SA-010**: Dr. Lin Chen's first formal research proposal, 2316. Submitted to the Terran Compact Colonial Authority. Rejected. Reason cited: "The proposal to study Void entities as potentially communicative organisms rather than as hostile fauna is premature and may undermine necessary military response." Chen's handwritten annotation on her copy: "They read the title and didn't read the proposal. The title says 'communicative.' The proposal says 'possibly.' There is a difference."

**SA-011**: First Fractured settlement record, 2335. Cpl. Daiko Wen's patrol log entry, quoted in full in faction-histories.md. The log that named the faction.

**SA-012**: New Amora Station Groundbreaking Record, 2334-11-01. Thessa Vorin's first on-site entry. The resonance sensors at the construction site measured 0.8847 Hz before construction began. Vorin noted this in her engineering log. She did not note it in the public groundbreaking record.

## Category 4: Technical and Scientific Records (Entries 36–47)

**SA-036**: Kesslerium Properties — Dr. Kessler's Final Paper, 2271. Published thirty years after her 2238 signal reception. "The element now designated Kesslerium exhibits resonance absorption at a rate 0.8847× more efficient than any other tested material. I have no theoretical explanation for this coefficient. I note, for the record, that this coefficient matches a signal I received in 2238 which I have not published about. I am 84 years old. I will not publish about it now. I leave it in this footnote for any reader who finds the coincidence as interesting as I do." — A. Kessler, *Kesslerium Properties and Applications*, footnote 47.

**SA-037**: Conclave Research CR-0847 — Entity Communication Attempts Summary, 2379. Five-year summary of all Conclave communication attempts with VE-Type IV entities. Total attempts: 847. Results with measurable entity response: 37. Results where entity response appeared intentional: 8. Results where entity response contained structured data: 1. (The structured data instance: 2378-08-47 is a clerical error for 2378-08-14. The data was the 0.8847 Hz overtone Rhae noted in CR-0047. It was a new variant. It is not yet understood.)

**SA-038**: Infrastructure Log INF-NOTE-012 — Session Timeout Decision, 2381. Full text: "We are setting session_timeout_idle to 1847 seconds (30 minutes and 47 seconds). Standard practice is 1800 (30 minutes exactly). The additional 47 seconds: (1) reduces abandoned session accumulation by an estimated 8.4% based on testing (players who step away briefly return within this window; the round 1800 seconds did not capture this behavior), (2) aligns with the station's NASD sequence number, which Seren pointed out after I chose the value for technical reasons, and which I am choosing to treat as a coincidence rather than a sign. — Karin Vogt, DevOps Lead. Addendum: Seren says 'it's not a coincidence.' I am documenting this disagreement. — K.V."

**SA-039**: API Design Document — Port 51847, 2384. "The port number 51847 was chosen because: it is in the unprivileged port range (1024–65535), it is not assigned to any known standard service, and it contains 1847 which is the station session timeout (and the NASD number, and apparently a number that just keeps appearing in this project). I am aware that choosing port numbers based on narrative significance is unusual. I am doing it anyway because there is no technical reason not to and because in five years these numbers will be in every configuration file and every new developer will wonder why, and someone will check the documentation and find this entry, and I want them to understand: we did this on purpose. Everything in this project is on purpose." — Ivan Chen, Backend Lead.

**SA-040**: The Harmony Hypothesis — Full Text, 2367. Elder Cassian Rhae's internal paper proposing that 0.8847 Hz appears in three independent physical contexts (Rift resonance, orbital mechanics, Ω-Space information transfer) because it is a fundamental resonance of the connection between normal space and Ω-Space. Full text available in Conclave Research Archive to players with Conclave Rank 5+. Summary available to all players.

**SA-041**: Vorin's 47 Equations — Compilation, 2387 (in-progress). Malo Fenn's ongoing compilation of all 47 equations, combining his derived 31 with the 16 hidden in the station's junction inscriptions (accessible via SQ-031). As of v1.3.2, players who have completed SQ-031 can view all 47 equations in Fenn's workshop. The equations describe: equations 1–31, standard architectural resonance engineering; equations 32–43, a broadcasting protocol for transmitting on 0.8847 Hz; equations 44–47, an address. "She built a transmitter and included the address of the receiver. Four equations. An address in Ω-Space." — Malo Fenn, in-game dialogue upon completing the compilation.

**SA-042**: Resonance Beacon Signal Log, 2381. The Fractured's first documentation of the Resonance Beacon signal. Technical parameters: frequency 0.8847 Hz (carrier), 2,847-character data burst, transmission origin outside known space. The log includes the Fractured's translation attempt (partial) and the coordinates that appear in Sermon 008.

**SA-043**: The 2,847-Character Transmission — Partial Translation, 2384. The Fractured's four-year translation effort, available as a collectible in the Beacon Site structure at Node 33. Provides: the coordinates of the transmission origin (outside the Kedraxis system; within FTL range); a phrase that repeats in the translated sections: "you were supposed to find this sooner." Does not translate: approximately 847 characters that do not match any known language structure. Solis's annotation: "The 847 untranslated characters are approximately 30% of the 2,847-character transmission. 30% of any message being untranslatable suggests either an encoding method we don't understand or a language component that has no analogue in our knowledge base. Given that this message appears to originate from a non-human source, I recommend accepting the latter explanation."

**SA-044**: Mowat's Folly — Design Documentation. The complete design history of VP-ITEM-00847. Full text in balance-history.txt Sprint 99 section and dev-notes.txt "The Folly Retrospective." Catalog note: "This item exists because a designer drew a formula on a whiteboard as a joke, was taken seriously by a CEO, and then had to design a weapon that justified the formula. The weapon is named Mowat's Folly. The designer is Seren Alyx Mowat. This is the best origin story in Void Protocol's development history." — Archivist Solis.

**SA-045**: ResFieldCalc's Reddit Post, 2024-09-11. Primary source of the tick rate bug discovery. Filed here as a historical document because it represents the first time the player community independently derived accurate game physics from observational data. Archivist Solis's note: "I find this event significant. ResFieldCalc derived the correct tick rate (64 Hz) and the correct EDR value (0.0337 per tick) purely from observation of cascade timing behavior. This is, in principle, the same method the Voidborn Conclave uses to study Void entities. Both are: observe patterns, derive rules, test against further observations. ResFieldCalc would make a good Conclave researcher."

**SA-046**: Community Reception — First 90 Days Internal Analysis. The full metrics document from Platform Operations. Key statistics: Peak concurrent 284,700 (launch day); D7 retention 41.2%; D30 retention 24.7%; Cascade events per session 0.84 average. The 0.84 cascade average was noted by the systems team: "We designed the EDR to create cascades every 7.72 minutes; the observed rate of 0.84 per session at 67-minute average session length implies 1 cascade per 79.8 minutes — which is higher than 7.72 minutes. Explanation: most players are not in sustained high-VRF combat for full sessions; the 7.72-minute figure is for continuous combat. Average real-world play has enough downtime to decay entropy before cascade. 0.84 per session is correct." — Tomas Reyes, Combat Designer.
