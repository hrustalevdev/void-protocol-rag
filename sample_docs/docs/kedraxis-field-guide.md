## Foreword

This field guide is the standard survey reference for explorers, salvagers, and researchers operating in the Kedraxis planetary system. It covers terrain, navigation hazards, Void Rift density maps, faction territories, and points of interest identified through the New Amora Station orbital surveys of 2380–2387.

Data is accurate as of 2387-01-01. Void Rift positions shift over months-long timescales; consult the Station Navigation AI for current rift positions before departure.

All VRF (Void Resonance Factor) readings in this document represent baseline measurements at ambient (non-surge) conditions. Active Void presence increases local VRF. Plan accordingly.

## Part I: Kedraxis-7 — Main Continental Survey

### 1.1 Overview

Kedraxis-7 is the seventh planet in the Kedraxis system — a mid-size rocky body (radius: 6,847 km, surface gravity: 0.94g) with a nitrogen-oxygen atmosphere suitable for unassisted human survival. Before the Void Rifts, K7 was home to 3.7 million colonists across fourteen settlements. After the Unraveling (2301–2315), those settlements were reduced to three survivors: Serethal (Conclave territory, population 47,000), the Iron Volley Fortress (Iron Sovereignty military installation, population 8,400), and the scattered Fractured settlements in the Northern Ruin region (estimated population 2,847 — the same number appears to recur without explanation).

**Average surface VRF**: 1.24 (elevated from baseline 0.8847 due to regional Rift density)  
**Combat classification**: Intermediate (recommended player level 25–50 for most surface zones)

### 1.2 The Serethal Settlement

**Population**: 47,000 (stable)  
**Faction**: Voidborn Conclave primary territory  
**Ambient VRF**: 0.91–1.10 (controlled; Conclave resonance suppressors active)

Serethal is the Conclave's primary surface settlement, built around the ruins of a pre-Unraveling administrative center. The Conclave's resonance suppressor network maintains VRF at 0.91–1.10 throughout the settlement perimeter, making it one of the few surface locations where Void entities rarely approach. The suppressors work by broadcasting an anti-phase signal at 0.8847 Hz, canceling the local Rift resonance.

**Settlement Districts:**
- **Research Quarter**: The Conclave's primary laboratory complex. Houses the Resonance Archive (largest collection of Void entity observational data outside New Amora Station), the Materials Lab (Kesslerium processing and alloy synthesis), and the Xenobiology Wing (restricted access: Conclave Rank 7+ required).
- **Market District**: Serethal's primary trade hub. Goods from the Shattered Belt salvage operations flow through here. Notable vendor: Yeva Cord (no relation to Veysa Ord from New Amora Station, though players frequently ask).
- **Residential Blocks**: 12 residential zones built on the original pre-Unraveling foundations. Structurally sound, though older districts show signs of the Unraveling's damage.
- **The Memorial Field**: An open park commemorating the estimated 3.7 million colonists lost in the Unraveling. A central obelisk lists 47 named victims — one representative from each of the original fourteen settlements. The remaining names are recorded in the Serethal Archive (Conclave Access only).

**Key NPCs in Serethal:**
- Archivist Yuna Tal: Side quest giver; connects to the "Voices in the Data" quest chain. Reward: Conclave Standing +847, access to Xenobiology Wing archives.
- Engineer Solvay Drek: No relation to Marshal Drek Volhari. Provides Kesslerium upgrade services. Will comment on the name coincidence if asked.
- Elder Cassian Rhae (visiting): Rhae divides his time between Serethal's Research Quarter and New Amora Station's Communion Spire. He can be found in Serethal on a 3-day rotation cycle. When present, he offers the "Resonance Study" daily quest (reward: 37 Void Shards + Conclave EXP).

**Serethal Quests (summary):**
- MSQ tie-in: "The Conclave's Evidence" (MSQ-003, part of main story)
- SQ-009: "47 Letters" — find 47 pre-Unraveling personal letters scattered across Kedraxis-7 zones, return to Archivist Tal. Reward: Unique cosmetic — "Remembrance Cloak" + Conclave Standing +2,000
- SQ-011: "What Vorin Left Behind" — follow maintenance corridors in Serethal's old administrative center to find a hidden inscription. This is a teaser quest for the broader Vorin mystery.
- Daily: "Resonance Calibration" — assist Conclave researchers in recalibrating suppressor nodes (3 nodes in the Market District + Research Quarter). Reward: 47 Void Shards

### 1.3 The Iron Volley Fortress

**Population**: 8,400 active personnel; additional support staff estimated at 1,200  
**Faction**: Iron Sovereignty military installation  
**Ambient VRF**: 0.72 (suppressed; Sovereignty VRF dampers active)

The Iron Volley Fortress was constructed over seven years (2309–2316) on the ruins of Hartmere, formerly the largest settlement on K7. The Sovereignty selected this site because Hartmere's elevation (highest point on Serethal Continent) provides unobstructed lines of sight to 84% of the continent's Void Rift distribution.

**Fortress Sections:**
- **Command Tower**: 47-floor administrative and strategic operations center. Marshal Drek Volhari's tactical situation room is on floor 37. Notable feature: the view from floor 47 shows all 14 original settlement ruins, weather permitting.
- **Armory District**: Weapons, Void energy shielding, and Kesslerium-reinforced armor manufacturing. Provides Iron Sovereignty commissioned gear to players at faction rank "Iron Marshal" and above.
- **The Memorial Wall**: By tradition, every Iron Sovereignty soldier who dies in Void Rift operations is named on this wall. Current names: 18,470 (a number that staff have noted contains 847 without acknowledging it publicly).
- **The Training Range**: Open to all Iron Sovereignty faction members of rank "Iron Soldier" and above. Provides combat simulation against VE-Type I and II entities at scaled difficulty. Clearing the Iron Veteran difficulty (VE-Type II, 3-wave) rewards 847 Void Shards.

**Key NPCs at Iron Volley:**
- Marshal Drek Volhari: Major story NPC. Combat trainer (highest-level techniques). Provides unique faction quests at "Iron Commander" rank.
- Lieutenant Commander Jenn Alcott: Survived the Volhari Outpost Incident with Volhari. Now his second in command. Faction quest giver: "The Node 12 Truth" (unlocks at Iron Rank 5; reveals classified aspects of the Volhari Outpost Incident).
- Quartermaster Ssemakula: Also a Volhari Outpost survivor (Cpl. Tom Ssemakula, now promoted). Provides unique Sovereignty gear crafting services. Will speak about the Incident if the player has the "Survivor's Record" key item (obtained from Jenn Alcott's quest).

**Iron Volley Quests (summary):**
- MSQ tie-in: "The Sovereignty's Position" (MSQ-004)
- SQ-017: "The Wall's Last Name" — investigate the death of the 18,470th soldier on the Memorial Wall. The circumstances are unusual. Reward: Iron Sovereignty Standing +1,847, unique weapon skin
- SQ-022: "Bulwark, Classified" — Iron Sovereignty Standing Rank 8 required. Access to classified Bulwark Station files. (This quest deliberately ends at a read-only door. Resolution: v2.0.)
- Daily: "Rift Patrol" — accompany an Iron Sovereignty patrol through one of three patrol routes on K7's surface. Guarantee: 1 Rift encounter per run. Reward: 47 Void Shards + Iron EXP

### 1.4 The Northern Ruin Region

**Classification**: Contested territory — no faction control  
**Ambient VRF**: 1.87–3.12 (highly elevated; multiple unstabilized Rifts)  
**Combat classification**: Advanced (recommended player level 50–75)

The Northern Ruin is what remains of seven settlements that were destroyed in the first three years of the Unraveling (2301–2304). The Rift density is high enough that the Conclave's suppressor network cannot maintain coverage, and the Iron Sovereignty considers the region outside defensible perimeter. The Fractured settlements here use a combination of pre-Unraveling underground bunkers and Void-adapted architectural techniques (learned from Conclave research) to survive.

**Sub-zones of the Northern Ruin:**

**1.4.1 Hartmere Ruins (Low-End Northern)**
VRF: 1.87–2.40 | Recommended level: 50–60

The ruins of Hartmere (the original settlement) that weren't incorporated into the Iron Volley Fortress construction. Maze-like structural remains; excellent for cover-based combat. Frequent VE-Type II patrols; VE-Type III sightings every 8–12 hours (real time) near the Rift at grid NR-0337.

Noteworthy: The first Fractured settlement (47 occupants, per Cpl. Daiko Wen's 2335 patrol report) was at grid NR-0337. A small structure still stands at that location — the Fractured's oldest continuously occupied building. A plaque on the wall reads: "We are fractured. That's not a problem. That's who we are. — 2335."

**1.4.2 The Shattered Wastes (Mid-Northern)**
VRF: 2.40–2.80 | Recommended level: 60–70

Flat terrain with minimal cover; remnants of agricultural infrastructure. The danger here is open-ground engagement against Cascaders — VE-Type III entities with the Cascade Burst ability. At VRF 2.40, Cascade Burst damage is amplified significantly (see combat-system.md for VRF amplification formula). Survival strategy: prioritize VRF reduction buffs, maintain dispersion >18.5 meters at all times.

Notable landmark: The Broadcast Tower. Pre-Unraveling communications infrastructure still operational — somehow. The tower emits an FM-range signal on the side-band corresponding to 0.8847 Hz. The signal has been present since before the Unraveling. The Iron Sovereignty has investigated and deemed it "a pre-Unraveling automatic broadcast, content unknown." The Conclave has catalogued it as "potentially significant." The Fractured broadcast Null's Sermons through it.

**1.4.3 The Old City Core (Deep Northern)**
VRF: 2.80–3.12 | Recommended level: 70–75

The geographic center of old Hartmere, now the most Rift-dense urban zone on K7. Approaching the geographic center requires navigating through a maze of collapsed pre-Unraveling structures and active Void Rifts (7 simultaneous Rifts documented in the 2387 survey). VE-Type III entities are common; VE-Type IV Architects have been sighted twice in the entire survey history.

**Communion Spire**: The Conclave's most important site is in the Old City Core — counter-intuitively, given the extreme Rift density. The Spire is a pre-existing structure (construction date unknown; estimated pre-colonization) that happens to be at the geographic center of K7's highest-density Rift cluster. Conclave researchers believe this is not coincidence. Elder Rhae's hypothesis: "The Rifts clustered around the Spire because the Spire's natural resonance (0.8847 Hz) acts as a focus for Rift formation. The Spire came first. The Rifts followed."

The Spire itself is neutral territory by formal agreement among all three factions — the only such agreement that exists. Violation of Spire neutrality has occurred once in documented history: the Fractured operatives who entered in 2381, killing Thessa Vorin. All three factions condemned this. The Fractured leadership has publicly stated that the operation was unauthorized.

**Points of Interest in Northern Ruin:**
- NR-0337: Original Fractured settlement + active Void Rift (radius: 22.5m, elevated VRF at edge: 2.31)
- NR-0441: Mass grave site from 2302 Unraveling event. Conclave maintains a small memorial. Collectible "Record of the Third Day" found here.
- NR-0847: The broadcast tower. FM transmitter. Collectible "Tower Log — 2301-06-14" found here — dated ONE DAY BEFORE the First Rift.
- Communion Spire: Central story location. Unlocked at MSQ Chapter 3.

## Part II: The Shattered Belt

### 2.1 Overview

The Shattered Belt is the debris field of Kedraxis-4, a planet that fragmented approximately 200,000 years before human arrival (cause unknown; the Conclave's "long-range Void Rift theory" proposes that the first-ever Rift in this system shattered K4; this theory is not accepted by mainstream xenology). The Belt consists of approximately 2,847 catalogued objects larger than 100 meters in diameter, plus uncounted smaller debris.

**Belt VRF gradient:**
- Outer Belt: 0.88–1.20 (transitional; matching station ambient at Rift edges)
- Mid Belt: 1.20–2.40 (active combat zone)
- Inner Belt: 2.40–4.00+ (deep Rift territory; Architect-class entities present)

**Belt Map Key Statistics:**
- Catalogued Rift locations: 184
- Active Rift locations (2387 survey): 147
- Closed Rift locations (first-ever documented: 0 — no Rift has ever been confirmed closed)
- Rift opening events since colony founding: estimated 847 (±37)

### 2.2 Major Belt Nodes

**Node 4 — "Iron Station Bravo"**
Iron Sovereignty forward base; 340 personnel. Kesslerium extraction platform, repair bay. Only Iron Sovereignty members can dock without a 47-credit visitor fee (the docking levy referenced in the Sovereignty's founding documents).

**Node 7 — "The Waypoint" (Neutral)**
Multi-faction trading post; no formal governance. Population varies: 200–1,500 depending on salvage cycle. Key vendor: Brax Vell (salvage broker; has connections to all three factions; affiliated with none). Standard exchange rate for Kesslerium raw ore: 3.7 credits per kg.

**Node 12 — "Volhari's Scar"**
Informally named for the Volhari Outpost Incident of 2372. Node 12 is now off-limits to Iron Sovereignty personnel below rank "Iron Marshal." All other factions may enter freely. Why the Sovereignty restricts its own forces: classified. Community speculation: the "5th survivor" from the incident's classified addendum was recovered from Node 12 and has never been publicly identified.

Current status: Active research node jointly operated by the Conclave and (unofficially) the Fractured. VRF readings: 1.87–2.31 at grid coordinates SB-N12-447293. The Rift that opened in 2372 is still active (radius: 18.5 meters, VRF at edge: 2.31). No attempt to close it has been made or announced by any party.

Conclave research notes on Node 12 (public access):
> "Node 12's Rift stability profile is unusual. Most Rifts expand by 0.5–1.5m per year. Node 12's Rift has maintained a constant 18.5m radius for 15 years (2372–2387). No mechanism for this stability has been identified. The standard physics of Rift dynamics would predict expansion to 25–30m by now. Proposed hypothesis: the Rift is being held at this exact radius by an external signal. The signal's source, if it exists, is inside the Rift." — Dr. Yuri Okafor, 2387-01-03

**Node 19 — "The Quiet Spot"**
No faction presence. High salvage value (decomposed K4 planetary core material); dangerous approach. VRF: 3.7+ throughout. One of the few Belt locations with confirmed VE-Type IV Architect sightings (3 in 2387 survey). Access requires Shattered Belt Expedition License (purchasable at New Amora Station for 1,847 credits).

**Node 33 — "The Beacon Site"**
The location identified in Null's Sermon 008. Not publicly known to be at Node 33 — the Fractured's coordinates encode to SB-N33-000847. The structure referenced in the sermon is present but invisible on standard scanning (Void frequency shielding: the structure only appears on 0.8847 Hz resonance scanners, which are Conclave equipment).

Accessing the structure requires: (1) Resonance Scanner (Conclave Rank 6 item), (2) Shattered Belt Expedition License, (3) completion of all 8 Null Sermon collectibles. The 37-minute recording referenced in Sermon 008 is found inside. It is currently untranslated in-game; translation is a v3.0 story beat.

### 2.3 Belt Salvage Operations

The Shattered Belt's primary economic activity is Kesslerium extraction from K4 debris. Kesslerium is the rarest element in the Kedraxis system (estimated total deposits: 847 metric tons; extraction rate: approximately 37 metric tons per year as of 2387; projected depletion in 22.9 years).

**Kesslerium Properties:**
- Resonance absorption: Kesslerium absorbs Void resonance energy at a rate 0.8847× more efficient than any other known material. This is why it is used in shields, armor, and Rift-suppression equipment.
- Market value: 47 credits per gram (standard; fluctuates ±20% based on faction conflict levels)
- Export value: Currently the Kedraxis system's primary export to Terran Compact territories

**Faction Kesslerium Policies:**
- Iron Sovereignty: Claims 37% of all Kesslerium extracted from Belt territory within their declared jurisdiction (roughly the outer two-thirds of the Belt by volume)
- Voidborn Conclave: Receives an equal portion via a 2341 treaty with the Sovereignty (the price of allowing New Amora Station's construction)
- Fractured: No formal claim; individual salvagers operate independently; suspected of intercepting Sovereignty shipments

**Player Salvage Mechanics:**
Players with Mining Class certification (available at Station Merchant Row) can extract Kesslerium ore from Belt nodes. Base extraction rate: 0.8 kg/hour per Tier 1 extractor. High-tier extractors add efficiency multipliers.

Note: Mining in Node 12 is unrestricted for all factions except Iron Sovereignty personnel, but the Node 12 VRF makes unshielded extraction risky. Experienced salvagers report that staying within 3.7 meters of a deployed Resonance Anchor reduces local VRF by approximately 0.84 units.

## Part III: New Amora Station — Detailed Layout

### 3.1 Station Architecture Overview

New Amora Station is a hexagonal ring structure designed by Chief Architect Thessa Vorin (2334–2341). The station consists of:
- 6 primary rings, each 18.5 meters in interior diameter at key junction points
- 3 main docking spurs (Alpha-1 through Alpha-3, for large vessels; Delta-7 for small craft)
- 1 central hub (the Core, housing the Resonance Chamber, primary AI systems, and administrative offices)
- 84 residential modules distributed across all six rings
- Total habitable area: approximately 84,000 square meters

Vorin's architectural intent (from her recovered personal notes): "A structure where no corridor leads to a dead end. Wherever you walk, you will arrive somewhere useful. This is both architectural principle and metaphor."

Station population: 47,000 residents + floating population of 5,000–15,000 transients.

### 3.2 Level-by-Level Directory

**Level 1 — Docking and Customs**
Primary arrival level. Contains Docking Rings Alpha-1 through Alpha-3 and Delta-7. New Amora Station Docking Authority (NASD) administrative offices. All players spawn here on first arrival.

- NASD-1847 Assignment Office: Players receive their assigned docking ID (NASD-XXXX format) upon first arrival. The sequence number is not explained in-game but the IDs 1848, 1849, etc. suggest NASD-1847 was assigned to a notable prior arrival. (It was Thessa Vorin. Her docking assignment on the day she arrived to oversee station construction is the origin of the NASD-1847 motif.)
- Customs Inspection Bay: Standard Compact clearance for off-world arrivals. Faction affiliation is registered here.
- Merchant Row Preview: The first vendor NPC the player encounters (Veysa Ord at her station entrance booth) provides the optional tutorial dialogue.

**Level 2 — Merchant Row**
Primary commercial hub. 47 vendor stalls (the count is deliberate; station administration requires 47 active vendor licenses at all times per Vorin's original commercial charter).

Notable vendors:
- Veysa Ord: Ship parts, general salvage supplies, gossip
- Malo Fenn's Workshop: Engineering and system diagnostics
- The Resonance Emporium: Void energy equipment, Conclave-licensed technology
- Iron Exchange: Iron Sovereignty faction equipment (requires standing)
- Fractured Supply Drop: Unmarked kiosk; changes location every 6 hours; sells unique items not available elsewhere

**Level 3 — Residential**
Housing for long-term residents. Players with sufficient credits (2,847+) can rent temporary lodging here. Permanent residency requires Neutral Standing 3 with the station's administrative council.

**Level 4 — Administrative and Medical**
- Station Administrator's Office: Governing body. Regular faction negotiations occur here.
- Station Medical: Full treatment services. Also where the Iron Sovereignty's medical examination of Null Osei took place in 2369 (the relevant medical file is a collectible found in the Medical Archives, restricted to Iron Sovereignty Rank 6+).
- Data Archivist Solis's Terminal: Primary location for Solis interaction. 24/7 availability.

**Level 5 — Research and Infrastructure**
- Conclave Research Annex: Smaller satellite to their primary Serethal facility.
- Engineering Core (Chief Fenn's domain): Monitoring station for all station systems. Malo Fenn is here 20/6 (leaves for 4 hours). He will literally ask players to leave because he's busy, then apologize, then invite them back.
- Resonance Monitoring Station: Public-facing VRF data for all nearby zones.

**Level 6 — The Resonance Chamber (Restricted)**
Access via main elevator from Level 5 (requires Neutral Standing 5 or faction Rank 6+).

The station's most significant structural feature. Vorin's hexagonal ring design creates a resonance node at the exact center of the station — the Resonance Chamber. The Chamber maintains a perfectly stable 0.8847 Hz VRF at all times. The mechanism by which it does so is partially understood (Malo Fenn has derived 31 of Vorin's 47 equations) and partially not.

Events in the Chamber:
- 2381: Fractured operatives breach the Chamber. Thessa Vorin killed. VRF stabilizer damaged but not destroyed.
- 2382–2387: Fenn's ongoing repair and equation-derivation work. Chamber operational but not at full designed specification.
- Current status: 0.8847 Hz maintained, but Fenn estimates the Chamber is operating at 84% of designed output.

Collectibles in the Chamber:
- "Vorin's Last Inscription" (on the eastern wall, barely visible — text: "Find the junction. Find the annotations. The full sequence will tell you what I built. I built it for you, whoever you are. — T. Vorin, 2340-08-12")
- Station Design Document Fragment (floor safe — combination is 0847; tells the player that Vorin's 47 equations are "not 31 of 47 but 47 of 47 if you count where they're hidden")

### 3.3 Junction N-47 — Docking Ring Delta-7

The most significant hidden location in Void Protocol. Located at the intersection of Docking Ring Delta-7's inner wall and maintenance corridor N-47 (accessed by crawling through an unsealed maintenance hatch behind a vendor stall on Level 1).

At this junction, on the inner wall:
- A mathematical inscription, approximately 2 meters wide, etched in Kesslerium-infused ceramic
- The inscription is the first equation in Vorin's extended sequence — one of the 16 equations that Chief Fenn has not been able to derive

Completing Malo Fenn's questline "Equations 32 Through 47" requires the player to:
1. Find the first inscription here (SQ-031 trigger)
2. Follow a chain of 15 more inscriptions hidden throughout the station at other junctions (all numbered with 47, 37, or 18 — the canonical numbers)
3. Photograph/scan each equation with the Resonance Scanner
4. Bring all 16 to Fenn

Fenn's reaction upon receiving all 16 equations is the most-watched video clip in the Void Protocol community: he stares at the equations for 8.4 seconds, then says quietly: "She didn't just build a station. She built a sentence." The quest rewards: "Architect's Commission" title, Conclave Standing +8,847, and the collectible "Fenn's Final Note" (M.F. — discovered to be Malo Fenn's own annotation confirming that Vorin was right).

### 4.3 Enemy Entropy Behavior Reference

All Void entities enter escalating behavior states as their entropy_level increases. The following table summarizes the state transitions as defined in the combat system (see VP-COMBAT-001 for full formulas):

| State | entropy_level | Behavior | Key Ability | Trigger Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dormant | 0.00–0.20 | Passive; no aggression unless attacked | None | — |
| Alert | 0.20–0.45 | Tracks player; does not attack | None | — |
| Aggressive | 0.45–0.70 | Attacks on detection | Standard attacks | Baseline |
| Frenzied | 0.70–0.90 | Continuous attack; ability spam | Type-dependent | +84% frequency |
| Cascade | 0.90–1.00 | Cascade Burst imminent | Cascade Burst (840 base) | Per 64 ticks after 0.90 |

Cascade state at 0.90+ is the primary danger for unprepared players. The 840 base damage of Cascade Burst reflects a deliberate design choice: at max health with T3 armor, a fully buffed Level 30 player has approximately 847 HP — meaning Cascade Burst at 0.90 entropy (base, non-VRF-amplified) will reduce them to exactly 7 HP. Seren Alyx Mowat called this "the 7 HP floor" — near-death but alive.

## Appendix: Survey Notation Key

**VRF Readings**: All measurements taken with standard Conclave resonance instruments calibrated to the 0.8847 Hz base frequency. Margin of error ±0.03.

**Coordinates**: KST (Kedraxis Standard Time) where time-of-survey is relevant. Grid references (NR-XXXX for Northern Ruin, SB-NXXXX for Shattered Belt nodes) are maintained by New Amora Station Cartography Division.

**Entity Classification**: VE-Type I (solo, reflex); VE-Type II (group coordinated); VE-Type III (strategic, ability use); VE-Type IV (Architect class). Type classifications established by Dr. Lin Chen and Dr. Yuri Okafor (2327); current standard across all factions.

**Rift Measurements**: Rift radius measured in meters at the event horizon edge. VRF at Rift edge is always 0.8847 at the moment of Rift opening; rises as Rift ages and expands. Current standard reference: Node 12 Rift = 18.5m radius, 2.31 VRF (15-year-old Rift with anomalous stability).
