INCIDENT — INC-20240905-001
Date: 2024-09-05 (Launch Day)
Severity: P1 (degraded launch)
Duration: 47 minutes (09:22 UTC to 10:09 UTC)
Reporter: Ivan Chen (Backend Lead)
Status: RESOLVED
=========================================

SUMMARY
Server capacity exceeded projections in first 2 hours of launch. NA West
and EU West regions experienced queue times of 8–22 minutes. Two of six
server regions (SA and APAC) had 99% capacity at 09:22 UTC.

TIMELINE
08:00 UTC — Void Protocol v1.0 servers go live
09:14 UTC — NA West reaches 94% capacity (projected peak was 78%)
09:22 UTC — SA region hits 100%; queue activated; P1 declared
09:31 UTC — Emergency capacity expansion initiated (cloud burst instances)
09:47 UTC — Additional 12,000 concurrent session slots online
10:09 UTC — All regions below 80% capacity; P1 downgraded to P2

ROOT CAUSE
Marketing predicted 180,000 DAU peak. Actual was 284,700 in first 3 hours.
Burst capacity plan was sized for 220% of marketing projection; actual was
158% of marketing projection.

CONTRIBUTING FACTOR
The "2,847 starting credits" tweet went viral at 07:47 UTC (1 hour before
launch). @VoidProtocolGame posted: "New players start with exactly 2,847
credits. That's the cost of a one-way docking permit at New Amora Station.
In the world of Void Protocol, you arrive with just enough." This generated
47,000 retweets and drove a second spike in player signup.

ACTION ITEMS
- Increase burst capacity to 250% of marketing projection for all future
  major content launches
- Implement dynamic capacity alerting at 70%, 80%, 90% thresholds (was 85%,
  95%, 100%)
- Review SA and APAC server provisioning (both regions underperformed
  relative to player base size)

LESSONS LEARNED
- "Non-round number" in early game is genuine marketing asset. The 2,847
  tweet generated more organic reach than the official launch trailer.
- Always size for 250% of marketing projection when the lead designer says
  "this number will generate discussion."

INCIDENT — INC-20240912-001
Date: 2024-09-12
Severity: P3 (minor service degradation)
Duration: 8 minutes
Reporter: Karin Vogt (DevOps)
Status: RESOLVED
=========================================

SUMMARY
v1.0.1 deployment. Deploy script had a configuration error: server tick
rate was set to 62 Hz instead of 64 Hz at deployment. Issue was caught
by community member "ResFieldCalc" on Reddit at 2024-09-11 (one day before
the hotfix was planned). Internal monitoring did not flag the 62 Hz value
because the alerting threshold was set at <60 Hz (too conservative).

TIMELINE
2024-09-05 08:00 UTC — v1.0 launches with 62 Hz (bug present from day 0)
2024-09-11 14:37 UTC — ResFieldCalc posts on Reddit: "Anyone else notice
cascade events are triggering 3.1% earlier than the math predicts? At
0.0337 EDR per tick at 64 Hz you'd get [correct calculation]. But I'm
seeing them hit at ~62 Hz equivalent. Server tick rate might be wrong."
2024-09-11 15:02 UTC — Internal team sees the Reddit post
2024-09-11 15:08 UTC — Ivan Chen confirms: "They're right. It's 62."
2024-09-11 15:12 UTC — P2 declared; hotfix prep begins
2024-09-12 09:01 UTC — v1.0.1 deployed; tick rate corrected to 64 Hz
2024-09-12 09:08 UTC — Verification complete; P resolved

ROOT CAUSE
Deployment script had a hardcoded TICK_RATE=62 from a QA environment
configuration that was accidentally used for production deploy. Production
config was correct (TICK_RATE=64 in server/config/production.yaml, line
47) but was overridden by the deployment script's environment variable.

IMPACT
All VRF and EDR calculations from 2024-09-05 to 2024-09-12 (7 days) were
slightly incorrect. Cascade events triggered approximately 3.1% early.
Analysis shows this affected approximately 0.3% of combat encounters in a
measurable way. No loot or economy data was materially affected.

ACTION ITEMS
- Delete hardcoded values from deploy scripts
- Add TICK_RATE to deployment validation checklist
- Set alerting threshold for tick rate deviation > 1 Hz
- DM ResFieldCalc from @VoidProtocolGame with a personal thank-you note and
  a package of 847 Void Shards (approved by Maricel Tran)

NOTE: ResFieldCalc received their Void Shards and posted: "I did the math
and 847 Void Shards at 0.0042 USD each = $3.56. I feel valued."

INCIDENT — INC-20241104-001
Date: 2024-11-04 (v1.1.1 deployment)
Severity: P2 (feature broken)
Duration: 18 minutes downtime + 6 hours discovery
Reporter: QA team (post-deployment)
Status: RESOLVED
=========================================

SUMMARY
v1.1.0 shipped a regression: Iron Stance was incorrectly redirecting enemy
attacks in solo mode. The solo modifier (which disables the co-op
redirection mechanic and only applies the 47% damage reduction to the
Warden themselves) was not applying.

DISCOVERY
Player "Ironclad_7" filed a bug report 6 hours after v1.1.0 launch:
"Iron Stance in solo mode is redirecting enemy attacks to... me? But from
range? I think it's trying to redirect attacks to an ally that doesn't
exist and defaulting to self-redirect. Taking way more damage than expected."

ROOT CAUSE
The solo/co-op mode check in combat-engine.ts was reading the session's
party_size field but the field was renamed from "party_size" to
"party_count" in v1.1.0 as part of the guild system prep work. The field
access returned undefined, which evaluated as falsy, which caused the
co-op code path to always execute.

FIX
Updated combat-engine.ts to use correct field name. 18-minute downtime
for v1.1.1 deployment. P2 resolved.

COMPENSATION
All Void Wardens who played v1.1.0 between launch and the fix received
47 Void Shards (approved by Seren Alyx Mowat: "47 is the Sovereignty
number. Wardens are Sovereignty-first. It's right.").

INCIDENT — INC-20241219-001
Date: 2024-12-19
Severity: P2 (economy exploit)
Duration: 4 hours active; discovery-to-fix 47 minutes
Reporter: Economy monitoring system (automated alert)
Status: RESOLVED
=========================================

SUMMARY
Economy monitoring flagged unusual Rift Exchange transaction volume at
03:22 UTC. Investigation revealed a dupe exploit: under specific timing
conditions (listing cancel within 0.8 seconds of a purchase completing),
an item could be duplicated — appearing in both the seller's inventory
(via cancellation) and the buyer's inventory (via purchase).

The 0.8-second window was not chosen accidentally by the exploiters —
they specifically identified the Phase Slip invulnerability duration (0.8s)
as a useful timing marker and used it to synchronize the cancel and
purchase requests.

IMPACT
- 847 duplicate items created
- 23 unique items that appeared in the game:
  * 312 common gear (Tiers 1-2): no material economy impact
  * 512 Tier 3-4 gear: moderate economy impact
  * 23 Tier 5 (Architect-Forged): significant
    - Notably: 12 Mowat's Folly duplicates (VP-ITEM-00847)
    - Mowat's Folly is supposed to be untradeable; the dupe exploit
      bypassed the tradeable check because the listing cancel occurred
      server-side before the tradeable flag was validated

TIMELINE
03:22 UTC — Automated alert: "Unusual duplicate item patterns detected"
03:24 UTC — Ivan Chen paged; begins investigation
03:28 UTC — Pattern identified: race condition in cancel/purchase endpoint
03:31 UTC — Feature flagged OFF (Rift Exchange maintenance mode)
03:39 UTC — Patch developed and tested
04:09 UTC — Fix deployed; Rift Exchange restored

ROOT CAUSE
Race condition between POST /v1/economy/exchange/listings/{id}/cancel and
POST /v1/economy/exchange/purchase. Both endpoints read the listing's
active flag before acquiring a row-level lock. Window between read and
lock acquisition (approximately 0.8–2ms) allowed concurrent requests to
both succeed.

FIX
Added SELECT FOR UPDATE on listing_id before any read in both endpoints.
Eliminated race condition.

Additionally:
- Untradeable check now happens at listing CREATION not at purchase
  (prevents items being listed at all rather than failing at purchase)
- Added rate limiting on cancel/purchase: max 5 combined actions per second
  per player (was previously uncapped within the API's 1,337/minute limit)

ACTION ITEMS (economy remediation)
- 847 duplicate items deleted from database
- 12 Mowat's Folly duplicates restored to UNTRADEABLE status; players who
  "purchased" duplicated Mowat's Follies had item removed and received
  full credit refund + 847 Void Shards compensation
- 23 accounts used in the exploit: 30-day suspension
- The 8 accounts that initiated the exploit (identified by timing patterns):
  permanent ban, per Terms of Service Section 7.3

Seren's note: "The exploiters used the 0.8-second Phase Slip duration as
their timing signal. I can't decide if I'm impressed or just tired. Both."

INCIDENT — INC-20250228-001
Date: 2025-02-28 (Emergency Hotfix)
Severity: P1 (critical exploit)
Duration: 12 minutes downtime
Reporter: Community report + internal monitoring
Status: RESOLVED
=========================================

SUMMARY
Two-player exploit combining Shadow Operative's Void Contract with Rift
Channeler's Cascade Burst allowed combined damage amplification of
approximately 47× intended maximum. This enabled the pair to kill any
Architect in the game in under 4 seconds (fastest was Null-Form Theta:
3.7 seconds).

DISCOVERY
Community video posted at 2025-02-27 23:14 UTC showing two players
killing The Hollow King (HP: 184,200) in 3 seconds. Internal team flagged
it within 22 minutes.

ROOT CAUSE
Void Contract's amplification was applying to the TOTAL damage of Cascade
Burst, including the VRF multiplier component that was calculated before
the amplification cap was applied. Correct behavior: amplification applies
to base damage before VRF scaling. Buggy behavior: amplification applied
after VRF scaling, compounding the two multipliers multiplicatively rather
than additively.

Expected maximum: 2.3× crit multiplier × 1.5 (max VRF amplification at
threshold) = 3.45× total. Actual with exploit: up to 47×.

FIX
Reordered calculation: base damage → Void Contract amplification →
VRF scaling → critical hit. Added explicit cap at 3.45× combined.
Deployed v1.3.1 hotfix; 12-minute downtime.

PLAYER ACTION
Two accounts confirmed to have intentionally used and demonstrated the
exploit to the public (the video creators): 72-hour suspension per Terms
of Service. No permanent ban because they disclosed publicly rather than
exploiting privately.

Seren's note: "47 times. Of course it was 47. The Iron Sovereignty motif
appears even in exploit damage numbers. I'm going to lie down."

Corrected: Phantom Protocol's critical multiplier was also adjusted from
3.7× to 3.4× to prevent a secondary exploit where the same interaction
could be reproduced with Shadow Operative's ultimate.
