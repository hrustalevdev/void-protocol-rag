VOID PROTOCOL — BALANCE HISTORY LOG
Darkfield Interactive | Systems Design Team
Lead: Seren Alyx Mowat
Document ID: VP-BAL-HIST-001
Period Covered: Sprint 47 (2022-03-07) through v1.3.2 (2025-02-28)
Classification: Internal Reference — Systems and Narrative Staff

This document captures the full history of balance decisions in Void
Protocol, including the reasoning behind key design choices and the
results of implemented changes. It is intended as a reference for
future designers and as a record that decisions made during development
were not arbitrary.

Key principle (from Seren Alyx Mowat, Sprint 47 kickoff):
"Every number is a decision. Every decision deserves a reason.
 If you can't write the reason down in one paragraph, you haven't
 finished deciding."

=========================================
SPRINT 47 (2022-03-07 to 2022-03-18)
"The Whiteboard Day Aftermath"
=========================================

Context: The "Whiteboard Day" was 2022-03-01. Seren Alyx Mowat drew
the VRF combat curve on the whiteboard — VRF from 0.0 to 5.0 on the
x-axis, damage multiplier on the y-axis. The key parameters:
  - VRF_BASE = 0.8847 (the only fixed point on the curve)
  - VRF amplification begins at: 1.0
  - VRF amplification cap at: 2.0 (multiplier = 1.5×)
  - Cascade trigger threshold: 0.9

The question in Sprint 47: are these numbers right?

SIMULATION RESULTS (Sprint 47):
Ran 10,000 combat simulations for each of the 6 character classes.

Finding 1: At VRF_BASE = 0.8847, all 6 classes achieve between 3.8
and 4.2 kill times against a standard VE-Type II entity. The variance
is 10.5% — meaning no class is dramatically faster or slower at base.
This is the design goal. Result: KEEP VRF_BASE = 0.8847.

Finding 2: The cascade trigger at entropy_level = 0.90 produces a
Cascade event approximately every 7.8 minutes in sustained combat at
VRF_BASE. At 64 Hz tick rate, the EDR of 0.0337 per tick means:
  0.0337 per tick × 64 ticks/second = 2.1568 entropy per second
  0.90 (cascade threshold) / 2.1568 = 0.417 seconds to first cascade
  That's wrong — it's way too fast.

Actually: EDR = Entropy Decay Rate, not Entropy Accumulation Rate.
Entropy accumulates from combat; EDR decays it. The actual accumulation
comes from combat damage dealt and received. The 7.8-minute figure
assumes a steady combat flow with 10 HP damage events per second.
At that rate, entropy_level hits 0.90 after 7.77 minutes.

Result: This is correct. The 7.72-minute average (from live data later)
matches the simulation. No change to EDR = 0.0337.

Finding 3: The starting credits value (2,847) was validated against
the docking permit lore. Maricel Tran approved. "This should never
change. It's not a gameplay value, it's a world value." — M. Tran.

Decision Log — Sprint 47:
- VRF_BASE: 0.8847 CONFIRMED
- EDR per tick: 0.0337 CONFIRMED
- Cascade threshold: 0.90 CONFIRMED
- Starting credits: 2,847 CONFIRMED
- Session timeout: 1,847 seconds CONFIRMED (infrastructure decision,
  see INF-NOTE-012; the value was chosen by Karin Vogt and happened
  to match the station's NASD number — this was noticed by Seren and
  added to the canonical numbers list retroactively)

SPRINT 52 (2022-05-09 to 2022-05-20)
"Class Identity Sprint"
=========================================

The six character classes were defined conceptually in Sprint 40. Sprint
52 was dedicated to finalizing their stat blocks. The key design
principle: "Each class should have exactly one answer to 'what do I do
in a Void Surge event?'" — Seren Alyx Mowat.

VOID WARDEN (Tank/Off-tank)
Design intent: highest survivability, lowest damage output, best group
utility. "The class that makes everyone else better."

Sprint 52 stats (finalized):
  BASE_HP: 1,200 (highest of all 6 classes)
  BASE_VRE: 150 (sufficient for utility; not enough for DPS)
  BASE_DAMAGE: 100 (lowest of all classes)
  Iron Stance: 47% damage reduction to others (group); self-only in
               solo mode. Duration: 8.4 seconds. CD: 24s.

Rationale for Iron Stance numbers:
- 47% chose because Iron Sovereignty motif; Void Wardens are coded
  as the "Sovereignty-first" class (see narrative guide). 47 is their
  number. 50% would be too strong for a 24-second cooldown.
- 8.4 seconds duration: long enough to matter; short enough that
  activation timing is skill. 8.4 = 0.84 × 10, and 0.84 is the
  VRF modifier at the amplification curve inflection point. Hidden
  connection to VRF math; probably no player will notice; we still
  like it.
- 24-second cooldown: prime number adjacent; 3× the cascade event
  window at 64 Hz. Chosen for mathematical elegance.

RIFT CHANNELER (DPS/Burst)
Design intent: highest single-hit damage; fragile; requires VRF
management; "the class that plays the VRF like an instrument."

Sprint 52 stats (finalized):
  BASE_HP: 750 (lowest of all classes; equivalent to a Drifter)
  BASE_VRE: 200 (highest VRE; needs it for Resonance Surge spam)
  BASE_DAMAGE: 180 (highest single-hit; VRF multiplier compounds this)

Design note on 750 HP: The Channeler has the same HP as a standard
VE-Type I Drifter (840 HP in the final design). Wait — this was Sprint
52; Drifter HP was set at 840 in Sprint 58. At Sprint 52, Drifter HP
was 750. So the Channeler was designed to be "as fragile as the weakest
enemy." When Drifter HP was raised to 840 in Sprint 58, the Channeler
was NOT raised to match — intentionally. "Players should notice that
they're more fragile than a Drifter. That's the feel." — Seren.

Resonance Surge: 8.0-second duration, 37 VRE/second drain, +84%
damage amplification. Visible glow at 8.4 meters (conspicuous to
enemies; balance cost of using it).
  [Note: 37 VRE/second × 8 seconds = 296 VRE total cost. A Channeler
  with max VRE (200 base) can sustain it for 5.4 seconds before
  exhaustion. This is a design limitation: Resonance Surge cannot be
  sustained at full power unless VRE is supplemented by external
  sources or Resonance Vents. — added to documentation Sprint 64]

PHASE BREAKER (Mobility/Off-DPS)
Design intent: highest movement speed; Phase Slip survivability; "the
class that's always somewhere the enemy didn't expect."

Sprint 52 stats (finalized):
  BASE_HP: 900
  BASE_VRE: 170
  BASE_DAMAGE: 140
  MOVE_SPEED: +37% above base (highest of all classes)
  Phase Slip: 0.8-second invulnerability + 18.5m teleport forward.
              CD: 37 seconds. VRE cost: 37.

Rationale for Phase Slip numbers:
- 0.8 seconds: precisely chosen as slightly less than the 0.8847 VRF
  base (thematic; "the Phase Breaker exists slightly outside the
  resonance of the world")
- 18.5 meters: exactly the Cascade Burst radius. Phase Slip escapes
  a point-blank Cascade. This is intentional and documented in DDL-003.
- 37-second cooldown: Fractured motif; Phase Breakers are the
  "Fractured-coded" class. 37 is their number.
- 37 VRE cost: same as cooldown. "The ability costs what it buys."

SHADOW OPERATIVE (Stealth/Burst)
Design intent: ambush gameplay; highest single-round damage potential;
"the class that wins before the enemy knows the fight started."

Sprint 52 stats (finalized):
  BASE_HP: 850
  BASE_VRE: 180
  BASE_DAMAGE: 160
  Stealth CD: 18.5s (passive recharge, triggers 1.5s after breaking)
  Phantom Protocol: 3.7-second stealth + 3.7× crit multiplier on
                    first strike. VRE cost: 84.

Note on Phantom Protocol multiplier: 3.7 is the Fractured motif (Phase
Breaker and Shadow Operative share it; Shadow Operatives are the covert
complement to Phase Breaker mobility). The 3.7× crit multiplier was
reduced from 4.0× in Sprint 58 after simulation showed it was too
strong in 2v1 scenarios. It was temporarily bugged back up in v1.3.0
(the exploit; see INC-20250228-001) and corrected to 3.4× in v1.3.1.

History of Phantom Protocol multiplier:
  Sprint 52: 4.0× (design intent)
  Sprint 58: 3.7× (balance pass; too strong)
  v1.3.0: 3.7× (shipped correctly)
  v1.3.0 exploit: effective 47× (due to Cascade Burst interaction bug)
  v1.3.1 hotfix: 3.4× (reduced 0.3× below pre-exploit as correction)

RESONANCE ENGINEER (Support/Utility)
Design intent: enables other classes; "the class that makes the
impossible fight possible, but takes none of the credit."

Sprint 52 stats (finalized):
  BASE_HP: 1,050
  BASE_VRE: 160
  BASE_DAMAGE: 110
  Resonance Vent: place a device that emits VRF suppression field
                  (radius: 3.7m); lasts 47 seconds; restores 18.5
                  VRE/second to all allies within radius.
  Harmonic Pulse: AoE restore; 37 HP/second for 8.4 seconds to all
                  allies within 18.5 meters.

Note: All Engineer abilities use the canonical numbers (3.7m,
47 seconds, 18.5m, 8.4s). This was a deliberate design choice:
"The Engineer works with the geometry of the world. Their numbers are
the world's numbers." — Seren.

ENTROPY WARDEN (DPS-ramp/Sustained)
Design intent: slow start, massive sustained output; "the class for
players who are patient and then devastating."

Sprint 52 stats (finalized):
  BASE_HP: 1,000
  BASE_VRE: 165
  BASE_DAMAGE: 130 (lowest true DPS at start; highest after ramp)
  Void Harvest: passive; generates 18.5 VRE per kill; stacks for
                8.4 seconds. After 5 stacks: Entropy Surge activates
                (damage ramp: +37% per stack, max +185%).
  Entropy Surge: 8.4-second window of accumulated damage bonus.
                 All kills during surge extend it by 0.8847 seconds.

Note on 0.8847 second extension: This is a deliberate Easter Egg.
An Entropy Warden on a kill streak has their Surge duration approach
the VRF constant asymptotically (each kill adds a fraction of the
last extension). In practice, players rarely achieve more than 12
seconds of sustained Surge (the VRF constant is not the limit; the
combat difficulty is). But the math is there.

SPRINT 58 (2022-08-01 to 2022-08-12)
"Enemy Design Foundations"
=========================================

Enemy HP values finalized in Sprint 58:
  VE-Type I (Drifter): 840 HP
  VE-Type I (Skimmer): 320 HP
  VE-Type I (Lurker): 580 HP
  VE-Type II (Warder): 1,680 HP
  VE-Type II (Striker): 1,260 HP
  VE-Type II (Handler): 2,100 HP
  VE-Type III (Cascader): 4,200 HP (+ 840 HP shield)
  VE-Type III (Harrower): 3,360 HP
  VE-Type III (Void Sentinel): 5,040 HP
  VE-Type IV (Architect — see Architect designs): 40,000–200,000 HP

Why 840 for the Drifter?
The Drifter is the tutorial enemy. The number 840 was chosen because:
1. It's close to the player's starting HP across most classes (750–1200)
2. At base damage (100–180), most classes kill a Drifter in 5–8 hits
3. 840 creates the "7 HP floor" at Cascade damage (840 base damage;
   a well-equipped Level 30 player has approximately 847 HP with T3
   armor; they survive a Cascade with 7 HP, which is traumatic and
   instructive)
4. 840 × 1.0847 ≈ 912 (the Warder HP if you scaled by the VRF constant;
   we didn't, but it was noted that 840 × VRF ≈ next tier threshold)

The number 840 was not chosen to be close to 847. It was chosen for
reasons 1-3. That it's close to 847 is a coincidence that Seren noticed
and left a note about: "If this is coincidence, it's the kind of
coincidence that happens when you use the same numbers throughout a
design. That's fine. It's called coherence." — Seren, Sprint 58.

SPRINT 64 (2022-10-24 to 2022-11-04)
"The Architect Files"
=========================================

The four Architect encounters were fully designed in Sprint 64.
This was a 4-week sprint (double length; approved by Maricel Tran
because Architect encounters are the game's most important content).

ELDER WARFORM THETA-9
Design philosophy: "The encounter should make the player feel
observed. Not hunted. Observed."

HP: 184,200 (chosen as: 84 × 2,190; 84 being the combat-relevant
harmonic of 0.8847, and 2,190 being the number of days in 6 years,
the time Seren had been designing this game; chosen on a whim and
kept because no one found a reason to change it)

Actually the real reason for 184,200 HP: at maximum sustained DPS with
optimally geared Level 87 group of 4 (Level 87 because that's the
max level; see DDL-001 for why 87), the fight should last approximately
8-9 minutes. At 4-player combined peak DPS of ~350 damage/second
(across all classes, fully buffed), 184,200 / 350 = 526 seconds =
8.77 minutes. That's the actual derivation.

The 84 × 2,190 coincidence was noticed after the fact.

Phase mechanics:
- Phase 1 (100%–70% HP): Observation mode. Theta-9 stays at range.
  Attacks single targets. Tests: player positioning and VRF management.
- Phase 2 (70%–40% HP): Active mode. Theta-9 moves. Deploys 3.7-meter
  resonance shockwave (damage: 374 per pulse, once every 8.4 seconds).
  Tests: group movement coordination and Engineer ability timing.
- Phase 3 (40%–0% HP): Void Communion. Theta-9 "opens" — its resonance
  signature strengthens to 3.47 Hz (4× the base 0.8847). Local VRF
  spikes. Cascade events are now possible on any entity (including
  players). Full burn phase; survive the chaos.

THE HOLLOW KING
HP: 84,000 (straightforward: DPS check fight; the right answer is
simply having 1,000+ sustained group DPS)

Design note: The Hollow King is the only Architect encounter that is
a straightforward DPS race. All others have mechanics. The Hollow King
tests: can the group deal damage? If DPS exceeds 1,000/second across
the group (84,000 / 84 seconds = 1,000 DPS required), the King dies
before its Phase 3 enrage. If not, Phase 3 is a wipe condition.

The choice of 84,000 HP and 84-second burn window: "I wanted a fight
where the math is obvious. 84,000 HP. Kill it in 84 seconds.
1,000 DPS. Every player in the group can do the math in their head.
That's the challenge. Not the mechanics — the preparation." — Seren

WARFORM PRIME
HP: 147,000 (chosen as 3 × 49,000; 49 = 7², which is the closest
perfect power to the canonical 47; "a square root away from home")

The Warform Prime encounter is the only Architect fight designed to
be beaten differently by different class compositions. A group without
a Resonance Engineer cannot survive Phase 2's sustained Entropy Drain.
A group without a Phase Breaker cannot handle Phase 3's area denial.
A group without DPS cannot kill it before Phase 3. This fight requires
all class types to be represented.

WARFORM PRIME Phase 2 — Entropy Drain:
Every 8.4 seconds, Warform Prime drains 84 VRE from each player.
A Rift Channeler (200 base VRE) lasts approximately 2.4 rotations.
An Engineer's Resonance Vent (restores 18.5 VRE/second; 47-second
duration) completely counters the drain if maintained.

THE UNDIVIDED
HP: 200,000 (highest in game; four-target fight)

The Undivided is four synchronized VE-Type III entities that share
an HP pool. All four must die within 0.8847 seconds of each other.
If they don't, the first three revive at 184 HP (a nod to Theta-9's
HP without being too obvious). The 0.8847-second window is the game's
most precise timing requirement — named in community guides as the
"VRF Window" because it is, literally, the VRF constant as a timer.

The mechanic was originally a 1.0-second window (Sprint 64 design).
It was tightened to 0.8847 seconds in Sprint 74 after playtesting
showed the 1-second window was too forgiving.
Seren's Sprint 74 note: "0.8847 seconds for the VRF Window because
this number is the game. It is the tolerance within which everything
in this world either works or doesn't. Including the players."

SPRINT 74 (2023-03-06 to 2023-03-17)
"The 87 Decision"
=========================================

Why is max level 87?

Initial design: max level was 100 (standard; obvious choice).
Sprint 60: Maricel Tran proposed changing max level to reduce the
"long grind to round number" problem. She wanted the max level to feel
like an achievement, not a milestone.
Sprint 64: Seren proposed 80. "Still round. Not interesting."
Sprint 70: Tomas Reyes proposed 84 (the VRF harmonic). Seren: "Too
clever. Players will assume it's meaningful and then be disappointed
when it isn't more meaningful than being clever."
Sprint 74: Seren proposed 87. Rationale:
  1. Not round (players don't grind to 87 on autopilot)
  2. 87 = 3 × 29; both prime-adjacent (29 is prime); slightly weird
  3. 8 + 7 = 15; 15 = max VRE bonus per stat level; hidden link
  4. Level 87 players call themselves "87s" in community; this creates
     a unique community identity that "100s" would not have
  5. The max-level content description could say "Level 87 — the point
     where mastery and the Void become indistinguishable" which is
     a line Seren had written in her notebook for 3 sprints waiting
     for a max level that fit it

Approved: max level = 87. See DDL-001 in VP-GDD-001.

SPRINT 78 (2023-05-01 to 2023-05-12)
"The Economy Balance Pass"
=========================================

Void Shard value: 0.0042 USD.

This was established in Sprint 78 after extensive market analysis of
comparable live-service games.

Inputs:
  - Comparable games: average premium currency rate $0.01 per unit
  - Lower value = more Void Shards per dollar; "better value" feel
  - Lower value = more Shards needed for items; "grind" problem
  - Ideal: players can earn meaningful Shards through gameplay alone

The initial proposal was $0.005 per Shard. Ji Soo Park (Economy
Designer) revised to $0.0042 based on simulation: at $0.0042, the
median player reaches 1,000 Shards through gameplay in approximately
22 days of regular play. 1,000 Shards = a single cosmetic item.

"They should feel like they earned it." — Ji Soo Park, Sprint 78.

Why 0.0042 specifically?
"It's not a round number so the price-per-item math requires thought.
Thinking about the math makes you aware of the value. That's not
predatory — it's respectful. You should know what you're paying."
— Ji Soo Park.

Rift Exchange transaction fee: 4.7%
"4.7% because: it's between the common choices of 5% and 3%, it
signals that this is a thoughtfully-designed economy, and 4.7 contains
47 (Iron Sovereignty motif; the Rift Exchange is a regulated market,
and the Sovereignty regulates markets)." — Ji Soo Park, Sprint 78.

SPRINT 84 (2023-07-24 to 2023-08-04)
"Final Combat Numbers Lock"
=========================================

This sprint locked all combat values for production. No combat number
changed after Sprint 84 without a formal design review.

LOCKED VALUES (Sprint 84):
  VRF_BASE = 0.8847
  EDR_PER_TICK = 0.0337
  TICK_RATE = 64 Hz (target; deployment bug meant 62 Hz at launch)
  CASCADE_BASE_DAMAGE = 840
  CASCADE_THRESHOLD = 0.90 (entropy level)
  VRF_AMP_START = 1.0 (VRF above this begins amplifying damage)
  VRF_AMP_CAP = 2.0 (VRF above this is capped at 1.5× multiplier)
  CASCADE_VRF_BOOST = 18.5 (VRF units added to zone during Cascade)
  VRF_DESYNC_TOLERANCE = 0.05 (server/client VRF difference tolerance)
  SHIELD_REGEN_RATE = 8.4 HP/tick (at rest)
  SHIELD_REGEN_DELAY = 3.7 seconds post-damage

The Sprint 84 lock was preceded by 300 hours of QA testing (3 weeks,
5 QA staff). The bug that was NOT caught: the deployment script's
hardcoded TICK_RATE=62. The test environment ran at 64 Hz. The bug
was in the deploy script, not the game code. ResFieldCalc found it.

SPRINT 99 (2024-03-18 to 2024-03-29)
"Launch Prep Balance"
=========================================

Final balance adjustments before 2024-09-05 launch.

IRON STANCE (Void Warden):
- Duration raised from 7.0s to 8.4s (felt too short in user testing)
- Cooldown lowered from 30s to 24s (same reasoning)
- 47% damage reduction: unchanged. "This number doesn't move." — Seren

RESONANCE SURGE (Rift Channeler):
- VRE drain lowered from 40/s to 37/s (made the ability viable for
  shorter sessions; 37 aligns with Fractured motif because Channelers
  operate at the edge of what's stable, which is what the Fractured do)

PHASE SLIP (Phase Breaker):
- Teleport distance raised from 15m to 18.5m.
  "18.5 meters. The Cascade radius. It should be exactly the
  Cascade radius." — Seren. QA: "But 15m felt better." Seren: "Play
  it again knowing what 18.5 meters means." QA: "...okay, 18.5m."

PHANTOM PROTOCOL (Shadow Operative):
- Multiplier held at 3.7× (reduced from 4.0× in Sprint 58; kept here)
- Confirmed not to interact with Cascade Burst multiplicatively.
  [Note: this confirmation was incorrect. See INC-20250228-001.
  The interaction existed but was not triggered in QA scenarios
  because QA did not test same-timing cancel+cast in a party context.]

RESONANCE VENT (Resonance Engineer):
- Radius confirmed at 3.7m. "The most important distance in the game."
  See: Phase Slip, Mowat's Folly trigger radius, NPC proximity trigger,
  Cascade Burst radius = 18.5m (5 × 3.7m). "3.7 meters is the quantum
  of closeness in this game." — Seren, Sprint 99 retrospective.

VOID HARVEST + ENTROPY SURGE (Entropy Warden):
- Surge extension per kill: 0.8847 seconds (established Sprint 52)
- Confirmed cap at 8.4 seconds total Surge (no extension can exceed
  this). This creates a ceiling: even with infinite kills, Surge ends.
  This is intentional. "The Entropy Warden's power has a shape.
  0.8847 seconds per kill, ceiling at 8.4 seconds. 8.4 / 0.8847 = 9.5.
  So approximately 9-10 kills extends the Surge to max. That's the
  ramp. That's the Warden's game loop in one number." — Seren.

MOWAT'S FOLLY (VP-ITEM-00847):
Finalized in Sprint 99 after 3 prior attempts to get this item right.

History:
  Sprint 60: First design — passive triggers every 7 kills, deals
             84 base damage to nearby enemies. "Too weak and too
             random." — Seren
  Sprint 70: Second design — passive triggers every 7 shots, deals
             840 base damage at range 7m. "Too strong at 7m." — Seren
  Sprint 84: Third design — passive triggers every 7 shots, deals
             840 × 0.8847 base damage at range 3.7m. "This."

Final design (Sprint 99 lock):
  TRIGGER: every 7th shot (not kill; every 7th time you fire)
  DAMAGE: 840 × 0.8847 = 743.1 (rounded to 743 in engine;
          the 0.8847 coefficient is stored as VRF_BASE in config)
  RADIUS: 3.7 meters
  SCALING: scales with player weapon damage, not VRF
  NOTE: Untradeable. Item ID: VP-ITEM-00847.
        "The item ID contains the coefficient. That's the joke."
        — Seren, Sprint 99.

Why is the passive coefficient the VRF base?
"This weapon is called Mowat's Folly because the coefficient is
a number I derived in a panic during Sprint 47 after Maricel asked
me what 0.8847 would look like as a damage number, and I said 'like
this' and drew the formula on the whiteboard, and she said 'that's
a weapon' and I had to design a weapon that justified the formula
existing. So I designed the item backwards from the number. It was
a design folly. It became Mowat's Folly." — Seren Alyx Mowat,
design retrospective, 2024-11-02.

POST-LAUNCH BALANCE LOG
=========================================

V1.0.1 (2024-09-12) — HOTFIX
Changes: Tick rate corrected from 62 Hz to 64 Hz.
Impact analysis: all combat values retroactively correct; the 3.1%
drift was within normal variance for most players except the 0.3%
affected by cascade timing. No compensation required (too minor to
justify retroactive rewards; decision made by Maricel Tran).
Starting credits corrected: deployment had a bug that set
STARTING_CREDITS=3000 (from a placeholder in QA config). Corrected
to 2,847. Players who had already used their starting credits were
not affected. The tweet about the 2,847 credits had gone viral the
day before, and then the fix corrected a 3,000-credit bug back to
2,847 — which generated a second wave of community discussion.
"The bug became better marketing than the fix." — Maricel Tran.

V1.1.0 (2024-10-15) — "IRON RISING" (MAJOR CONTENT)
Balance changes: none to core values.
New content: 4 Iron Sovereignty faction quests (SQ-034 through SQ-037)
  New item: Iron Marshal's Sidearm (VP-ITEM-00334) — unique to IS rank 8
  New zone: Iron Volley's Underground (Level 60 content)

V1.1.1 (2024-11-04) — HOTFIX
Changes: Iron Stance bug (see INC-20241104-001). Corrected field name
party_count (was: party_size). Solo mode now correctly applies
47% DR to self only.

V1.2.0 (2024-12-03) — "VOID SURGE" (MAJOR CONTENT)
Balance changes:
  Cascade Burst radius: reduced from 18.5m to 8.4m.

  Reason: Player feedback indicated that at VRF > 2.0, the 18.5m
  radius made Cascade events nearly impossible to survive without
  Phase Slip. In zones with multiple enemies, overlapping Cascade
  events were creating guaranteed-death scenarios at VRF > 2.5.

  Simulation result: 8.4m radius produces 0.84 average cascade
  deaths per session in high-VRF zones (was: 2.3). Target was <1.0.

  Community reaction: significant pushback. Players felt 18.5m was
  distinctive and "iconic." The Fractured subreddit noted that 8.4m
  "destroys the 3× radius relationship" (18.5 = 5 × 3.7; 8.4 does
  not have a clean multiple of 3.7). Seren acknowledged this publicly.

V1.3.0 (2025-01-14) — "FACTION WARS" (MAJOR CONTENT)
Balance changes: none to core values.
New content: Faction Wars PvP mode; faction standing weekly resets.

V1.3.1 (2025-02-28) — EMERGENCY HOTFIX
Changes: Cascade Burst Void Contract interaction fixed.
  Calculation reordered: base damage → Void Contract → VRF scaling
  → critical hit (was: base damage → VRF → Void Contract → crit).
  Phantom Protocol multiplier reduced: 3.7× → 3.4×.

  Note on 3.4×: "I was going to keep it at 3.7 because 3.7 is right.
  But I reduced by 0.3 because the exploit used the 3.7 multiplier
  as a component, and I want to close every door. 3.4 is still
  strong; it's just not exploitable in the Cascade interaction
  because the math no longer works. 3.7 comes back in v2.0."
  — Seren Alyx Mowat, post-hotfix note.

V1.3.2 (2025-03-11) — BALANCE PATCH
Changes:
  Cascade Burst radius: raised from 8.4m to 7.2m.

  Wait — this is wrong. 7.2m is lower than 8.4m. Clarification:

  In v1.2.0, radius was reduced from 18.5m to 8.4m.
  Community feedback was mixed: many players still found 8.4m
  too large in solo high-VRF content.
  In v1.3.2, radius was further reduced from 8.4m to 7.2m.

  Rationale for 7.2m:
  - 7.2 = 72/10; 72 = 8 × 9; 72 is a highly composite number
    (this math was not the reason; it's post-hoc)
  - At 7.2m, Cascade events in solo Level 75+ content drop from
    "likely lethal" to "survivable with T4 armor and no standing
    in the radius"
  - The target: Cascade should be a significant threat, not a
    guaranteed wipe mechanic in solo content
  - At 7.2m: simulated survival rate for a Level 75 solo Void
    Warden (best case): 64%. Simulated survival for a Level 75
    solo Rift Channeler (worst case): 8.4%. This is intentional:
    Channelers are not supposed to survive Cascades in high-VRF
    zones without support. That's their class identity.

  Additional v1.3.2 changes:
  - Resonance Vent radius raised from 3.7m to 4.2m.
    (Community feedback: 3.7m was too precise to position in combat)
    Seren's note: "I increased it to 4.2 because it's not 5.0,
    which would feel generic, and it's not 3.7, which community
    testing says is too small. 4.2 ÷ 3.7 = 1.135. Not meaningful.
    Sometimes a number is just a number that works."

BALANCE PHILOSOPHY — RETROSPECTIVE
Seren Alyx Mowat, 2025-03-15
=========================================

I have been designing Void Protocol for five years. Here is what
I understand now that I didn't understand then.

Numbers are not arbitrary. This seems obvious. It is harder to
practice than to state.

The difference between a number that works and a number that's right
is: a number that's right means the same thing in every context it
appears in. 0.8847 is the VRF constant. It is also the damage
coefficient of Mowat's Folly (the one I designed backwards from a
joke on a whiteboard). It is also the Entropy Surge kill-extension
in seconds (0.8847 seconds per kill). It is also, apparently, the
orbital resonance ratio of Kedraxis-4 debris in the Shattered Belt
(our astrophysicist ran the numbers for the world bible and it came
out to 0.8847; we didn't design this; we just checked). It is also
the VRF reading at the edge of every Void Rift in the known universe.

A number that appears across this many independent contexts is not
arbitrary. It is a fundamental constant of the world we built.

I think this is the lesson of Void Protocol's design: if you hold
one principle constant — in our case, 0.8847 — and make every other
number in the world relate to it (through multiplication, through
derivation, through coincidence, through intention), then the world
coheres. The player feels the coherence before they identify it.
They feel like the world is real. That's the goal.

Max level is 87 because of many reasons. One of them is 8.7 ≈ 10 ×
0.8847 rounded. I noticed this after we chose 87. That's fine. The
design validates itself if the principles are right.

The one number I would change: Cascade Burst radius. 18.5m was right
as a design. It was wrong as a live-service decision. I should have
built the difficulty curve to accommodate 18.5m rather than reducing
the radius twice because players found it too hard. The reduction to
7.2m works, and I'm proud of the balance now. But I wish we had
found a way to keep 18.5m.

18.5 = 5 × 3.7. 3.7m is the quantum of closeness in this game.
When everything is built around 3.7m, a Cascade that reaches 5 ×
3.7m away feels right in your body. You feel the geometry.

7.2m just means: don't stand there.

Something is lost. Not much. But something.

The fix was right. The original was better. Both things are true.
