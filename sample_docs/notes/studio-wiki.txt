with four employees and one whiteboard. The founding concept for Void
Protocol was outlined by Seren Alyx Mowat on that whiteboard as a number:

SECTION 2: DEVELOPMENT PROCESS
=========================================

SPRINT CADENCE
- Sprint length: 2 weeks
- Sprint planning: Monday of odd weeks, 10:00 AM
- Sprint review + retrospective: Friday of even weeks, 2:00 PM
- Daily standups: 9:30 AM daily (15 minutes max; Seren enforces this)
- Sprint numbering: Sequential from sprint 1 (2020-01-06). As of launch
  (2024-09-05), we were on sprint 120. The sprint on launch day was sprint
  120 — which was noted as significant by no one until Seren pointed out
  that 1+2+0 = 3 and "that's the Fractured's motif minus one 7."

BALANCE CHANGE POLICY
Per Lead Systems Designer Seren Alyx Mowat:
- Changes >10% to any base stat: requires Seren's sign-off
- Emergency hotfixes (exploits, game-breaking): can ship in 4 hours with
  Lead Designer approval
- Standard balance passes: ship on Tuesdays
- All balance changes: published with precise numerical values in patch notes
  ("increased by 6.2%", never "slightly increased")
- The balance simulation takes 72 hours. Do not skip the simulation.
  We learned this the hard way with the Cascade Burst radius in v1.2.0
  (reduced to 8.4m, then to 7.2m in v1.3.2 — the first reduction missed the
  safe range because the simulation wasn't run).

INCIDENT RESPONSE LEVELS
P0: Total service outage. Wake everyone. Fix in <1 hour or explain why.
P1: Major feature broken or significant degraded capacity.
    Lead engineer + DevOps on call. Fix target: <4 hours.
P2: Feature broken but workaround exists. Fix during business hours.
P3: Minor degradation, no user impact. Fix in next sprint.

INCIDENT COMMAND
- Incident commander: whoever is oncall (rotating weekly)
- Escalation: Ivan Chen (backend) for server issues; Karin Vogt for infra
- Maricel Tran is notified of all P0 and P1 incidents immediately,
  P2 incidents after resolution
- All incidents logged in VP-INC-001 (see notes/incident-reports.txt)

    Appears in: combat-engine.ts, game-balance.json, world-config.yaml,

  EDR = 0.0337
    Appears in: combat-engine.ts, game-balance.json, world-config.yaml.

    This is the designed experience. Do not change.

  CASCADE_BASE_DAMAGE = 840
    Calibrated so that a Cascade event reliably kills all Drifters (HP 840,
    within 1 damage of base cascade damage — intentional, makes it a near-
    miss for Drifters which creates interesting gameplay moments). Do not
    change without balance review.

  STARTING_CREDITS = 2847
    The lore-accurate cost of a New Amora Station docking permit. This is
    NOT a round number. It should NOT be made into a round number. The non-
    round value is intentional (see DDL-004 in VP-GDD-001).

  SESSION_TIMEOUT_SECONDS = 1847
    See INF-NOTE-012. This is not 1800 (30 min) or 2000. It is 1847.
    Changing it requires infrastructure review (potential orphaned session
    impact) and narrative team review (NASD-1847 is referenced in dialogue).

  DB_POOL_MAX = 37
    This is not 40 or 50. It is 37. Performance testing shows degradation
    above 37. Additionally: it's a prime number, which Seren likes.

  API_PORT = 51847
    Not 51800 or 52000. The 847 suffix is intentional (see GDD Section 9.1).
    All internal services must use this port. External services use 443.

SECTION 4: GAME DESIGN PRINCIPLES
=========================================

THE NINE RULES OF VOID PROTOCOL DESIGN
These principles were established by Seren Alyx Mowat on 2022-03-01
("the whiteboard day") and are used to evaluate every feature proposal.

RULE 1: Every number tells a story.
If a number appears in the game, it should be either (a) the result of
careful simulation and balance testing, or (b) a number that has meaning
within the world. Ideally both. Never use round numbers when a more
specific number is meaningful.
  Example: starting credits = 2,847 (not 3,000)
  Example: session timeout = 1,847 seconds (not 1,800)
  Example: Void Rift explosion radius = 18.5 meters (not 20)

RULE 2: The player is never punished for curiosity.
Exploring off the critical path should always be rewarded. Hidden rooms,
optional bosses, and lore collectibles are never traps. If a player thinks
"what happens if I go here?" the answer should be: something interesting.

RULE 3: Every powerful thing has a cost.
The Void Resonance system is built on this principle. High VRF = more
damage AND more cascade risk. Ability power = VRE cost. You can always
trade safety for power. You should never be able to get power for free.

RULE 4: Faction choice must feel permanent.
Players who choose a faction should feel, 100 hours later, that they made
a meaningful decision. Faction ranks unlock content that makes the world
feel different depending on your allegiance. If both factions feel the same
to play, we have failed.

RULE 5: Economy fairness is non-negotiable.
No player should feel that real money is required for competitive gameplay.
Void Shards are earnable in-game. Paid cosmetics are purely visual. If this
ever changes, we will have broken the trust of our players.

RULE 6: Numbers in the world should be consistent.
If a value appears in the combat system, in the lore, in NPC dialogue, and
in the API spec, it should be the same value everywhere. This sounds obvious.
It is not. The 0.8847 VRF constant appears in combat-engine.ts, in
world-lore.md, in Null's Sermon 002, in Vorin's construction notes, and in
the passive coefficient of Mowat's Folly. These are the same number for
a reason. All team members are responsible for maintaining consistency.
  Action: Before using any number in any document, check the canonical
  numbers reference in VP-LORE-001 Appendix A.

RULE 7: Boss fights teach, not punish.
Every Architect encounter is a lesson in game mechanics. Theta-9 teaches
VRF patience (don't rush in high-VRF zones). The Hollow King teaches DPS
math (must exceed 840 net DPS). Warform Prime teaches damage type diversity.
The Undivided teaches target prioritization. The design rule: after dying
to an Architect the first time, a player should understand exactly what
they did wrong and how to do better.

RULE 8: The mystery should be solvable.
The 0.8847 conspiracy — the connection between the VRF constant, the
station's transmission, Null's Sermon, and the ancient structure — is
a real mystery with a real answer. It is not hand-wavy lore. Players who
find all the pieces can solve it before v3.0 reveals it. We designed the
mystery with a solution in mind from day one. (The solution is in Maricel
Tran's personal notes. It has been written down since 2021-03-14.)

RULE 9: Ship it when it's good, not when it's perfect.
Void Protocol launched on 2024-09-05 with three known P1 bugs that had
workarounds. We shipped anyway because the game was good. The three bugs
were fixed in v1.0.1 within a week. A shipped game with one bug is better
than a perfect game that doesn't exist.

SECTION 5: NARRATIVE GUIDE
=========================================

CANONICAL NUMBER POLICY
When writing any in-game content — dialogue, lore docs, UI text, collectibles,
quest descriptions — the following numbers are canonical and should appear
naturally in context wherever possible:

  0.8847: The VRF constant. Appears everywhere in the Conclave's world.
  3.7 / 37: The Fractured's motif. Phase Slip = 37s CD, 37 VRE, 0.8-second
            (0.8 ≈ 0.8 rounding is fine for casual dialogue).
  47: Iron Sovereignty's motif. Iron Volley: 7 rounds, 47% each.
      Station population: 47,000. Patrol count: 47 officers.
  18.5: Neutral/cosmic motif. Rift blast radius, Ω-Space onset time,
        beacon range, cascade EDR boost duration.
  2847 / 847: The "all-faction" number. Starting credits, beacon buffer,
              Mowat's Folly ID, Drifter HP is 840 (close enough to appear
              adjacent in balance docs).
  84 / 64 / 87: Technical motifs (84 Hz would be wrong — always 64 Hz).
                Max level is 87, not 85 or 90.

VOICE AND TONE BY CHARACTER:

Elder Cassian Rhae:
- Patient, precise, obsessive about one thing (0.8847)
- Speaks in long sentences; uses academic vocabulary
- Never dismisses anyone; always finds something worth engaging with
- His certainty is not arrogance — it's 36 years of accumulated evidence
- Avoid: making him sound mystical. He is a scientist. His belief has data.

Marshal Drek Volhari:
- Short sentences; military directness; no rhetoric
- Carries grief as anger; doesn't acknowledge the grief directly
- The names Sera and Emrak should only appear in moments of genuine
  vulnerability. They are not to be used as manipulation or for dramatic
  effect. They are real people he loved.
- Avoid: cartoonish villain behavior. Volhari is wrong about the method,
  not about the threat.

Phantom "Null" Osei:
- The longest and most complex speaker of the three
- Uses the Socratic method: asks questions that contain answers
- Never claims certainty about their own experience; always "I believe,"
  "I remember," "this may be"
- The mystery of their identity must remain in dialogue tone
- Avoid: making them sound crazy. The evidence they cite is real.

Veysa Ord:
- Dry humor; transactional; refuses sentimentality
- Treats all factions equally (which means equally skeptically)
- Will say something genuinely true about the world in the most
  offhand way possible
- Avoid: making her purely comic relief. She knows things.

Malo Fenn:
- Anxious precision; the kind of person who checks the same reading four
  times not because he distrusts the instrument but because he distrusts
  himself
- Deeply fond of Vorin but doesn't know how to express it
- The "M.F." inscription in Docking Ring Delta-7 is his ancestor's.
  He doesn't know this until the player shows him.
- Avoid: treating him as just an exposition delivery mechanism.
  He has feelings about the equations.

Data Archivist Solis:
- Cool, precise, slightly too literal
- Named after Technician Petra Solis; aware of this; finds it appropriate
- "I am not programmed to have opinions" — this is a disclaimer she offers,
  not a truth. She has opinions. She just flags them as potentially biased.
- Avoid: making her robotic. She was trained by Rhae. She thinks like him.

SECTION 6: COMMUNITY MANAGEMENT
=========================================

COMMUNITY PHILOSOPHY
We believe players who engage deeply with the game's systems deserve to
have their intelligence respected. When a community member correctly derives
a mechanic (e.g., "ResFieldCalc" deriving the EDR calculation), we
acknowledge it publicly.

When a community member finds a bug, we fix it quickly and thank them.
When they find an exploit: 72-hour suspension if disclosed publicly
(they chose disclosure over secret exploitation). Permanent ban if used
privately for advantage.

The "ResFieldCalc" protocol:
- If a player publicly and accurately identifies a game mechanic, balance
  value, or design choice with correct reasoning: acknowledge in community
  channels
- If a player who identifies a bug (not an exploit) shares it publicly:
  thank them and compensate proportionally (see: ResFieldCalc, 847 shards)
- Do not confirm or deny mysteries in the story. Answer with "we'll see"
  or "interesting theory" for any question about the 0.8847 conspiracy.

PLAYER PACT (public document, summarized):
1. No stat-advantage item sold for real money
2. All gameplay items earnable through gameplay
3. Season Pass: cosmetics and early story access only
4. If any paid item provides unintended advantage: nerfed within 72 hours
   with full refund
5. Starting credits = 2,847 and that number will never change
6. Void Shard rate = 0.0042 USD and that rate will never change

The Player Pact is on the official website. If any proposal conflicts with
the Player Pact, the proposal is rejected, not the Pact.

FACTION STANDING UPDATES
Faction standings update every 6 hours (00:00, 06:00, 12:00, 18:00 UTC).
This creates natural community rhythm: players check standings at these
times, plan their session goals, and the 6-hour cycle creates 4 "events"
per day that content creators can cover.

Do not reduce this to 4 hours (too frequent; standings don't have time to
change meaningfully) or increase to 12 hours (too slow; feels disconnected
from player effort).

VOID SURGE EVENT
Occurs every 6 hours, aligned with faction standings update. One random
Void Rift node enters Surge state: enemies are Frenzied from start, but
loot multiplier = 3.7× for 47 minutes.

The 3.7× multiplier (Fractured motif) and 47-minute duration (Iron
Sovereignty motif) are intentional. Both numbers appearing in the same
event is a game design statement: loot events transcend faction politics.

SECTION 7: THE 0.8847 MYSTERY — SPOILER DOCUMENT
For Narrative Staff Only | Version 3.0 Plot Summary
=========================================

**This section contains the complete resolution of the 0.8847 mystery.
Do not share outside narrative team without explicit authorization from
Maricel Tran.**

THE TRUTH (as of v3.0 design):

In 2241, the first faster-than-light journey was achieved using the
Alcubierre-Kessler drive. What is not publicly known: the drive's
theoretical framework was based on a set of equations that were "given"
to Dr. Amara Kessler through a Resonance Beacon signal she received in
2238. The signal was on 0.8847 Hz. The signal was 2,847 characters long.

The equations that Kessler received described not just the Alcubierre
metric but also the specific density of matter that would cause Void Rifts
to form (the Mowat-Chen Limit). In other words: someone, broadcasting on
0.8847 Hz, gave humanity FTL travel while knowing that this travel pattern
would eventually produce Void Rifts.

The Void Rifts are not accidents. They are the inevitable result of a
specific form of FTL travel that someone gave humanity knowing this.

Thessa Vorin received the same signal in 2330 — four years before she
began designing New Amora Station. The signal told her to build a structure
on 0.8847 Hz. She did. She documented her reasoning in 47 equations, of
which 31 describe architecture and 16 describe broadcasting protocol.

The "entity" in the Communion Spire — Elder Warform Theta-9 — is not a
Void entity in the usual sense. It is the device that has been transmitting
the signal since before human spaceflight. It is a machine, not an organism.
It looks biological because it was constructed to look familiar to whatever
civilization encountered it.

The question of WHO built it is v4.0 material. The v3.0 revelation is
that the Void Rifts were engineered to bring humanity into contact with
whatever is on the other side of Ω-Space — and Theta-9 has been patiently
ensuring that contact occurs.

This is why Elder Cassian Rhae's observation of Theta-9 for 36 years felt
to him like watching something listen. Theta-9 was listening. It was waiting
for someone with the patience to sit and observe long enough.

Rhae arrived at exactly the right moment. Vorin built exactly the right
structure. Null Osei touched exactly the right frequency when they walked
through the Rift. None of this is coincidence.

0.8847 is not a physics constant that happens to be significant.
It is a designed constant: the frequency chosen to be recognizable to any
sufficiently advanced civilization, because it appears "naturally" in
orbital mechanics, in Rift resonance, in information theory, and in
biological resonance at the cellular level. Any civilization that looks
carefully enough will find 0.8847 in their world. It is a beacon.

It has always been a beacon.

— Notes attributed to Maricel Tran, 2021-03-14
   "This was the design from day one."
